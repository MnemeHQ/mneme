# Mneme Product Vision

Mneme is evolving toward becoming the architectural governance layer for AI-assisted development.

Our long-term vision is to provide the constraint and governance infrastructure that helps teams ensure AI-generated code adheres to architectural and engineering standards across increasingly agentic development workflows.

---

## Product Evolution Roadmap

### Phase 1 — OSS Developer Wedge (Current)

Mneme begins as a local/open-source governance tool for individual developers and engineering teams adopting AI coding tools.

**Focus:**
- Architectural decision retrieval
- Constraint injection into coding workflows
- Governance benchmark proof
- Developer workflow integrations

**Goal:** Prove the value of governance-before-generation in AI-assisted development.

---

### Phase 2 — Team Governance Layer

Shared governance and policy management for teams adopting AI-assisted development at scale.

**Planned capabilities:**
- Shared policy / decision stores
- Centralized rule management
- Team synchronization
- Organization-wide governance controls

---

### Phase 3 — Agent Platform Integrations

Integration layer for enterprise coding and engineering agent platforms.

**Potential integrations:**
- Enterprise AI coding platforms
- Managed agent environments
- Internal engineering agent systems
- Agent orchestration frameworks

---

### Phase 4 — Governance Infrastructure

Core governance infrastructure for AI-assisted software development.

**Potential capabilities:**
- Policy-as-code for engineering agents
- Drift analytics and adherence reporting
- ADR lifecycle/versioning
- Cross-agent governance enforcement
- CI/CD and workflow governance hooks

---

## Guiding Principle

Mneme is being built to move governance upstream:

> From post-generation review → To pre-generation architectural constraint

---

## Current Focus

We are currently focused on validating the OSS wedge by proving:

- AI coding workflows benefit from architectural governance
- Governance-before-generation improves adherence to engineering decisions
- Teams adopting AI-assisted development need stronger architectural controls

---

> **Note:** Roadmap phases reflect current strategic direction and may evolve based on user feedback and adoption patterns.
